About FUE Hair Transplant

One of the most popular and effective hair restoration methods available today

In recent decades, hair transplant technology and surgical methods have advanced at an exponential rate. FUE is one of the most commonly used surgical hair transplant methods for extracting grafts for hair restoration.

Follicular Unit Extraction (FUE) involves surgically removing individual hair follicles from the donor area of the back of the head, where the hair is healthy and strong. Grafts are then extracted and implanted in the recipient area where hair is needed.

Our FUE differs from traditional FUE in that we combine it with our microneedle technology to maximize the number of grafts. This is beneficial when a client has thin or sparse hair in the donor area.

The FUE Procedure

The FUE procedure involves extracting individual hair follicles and implanting them in the recipient area.

1

Donor Area Preparation

The first step is to identify the donor area from which healthy and strong follicles will be extracted. Hair trimming occurs in the identified donor area to facilitate the extraction of individual healthy grafts.

2

Follicle Extraction

After identifying healthy follicles, these grafts are separated from the rest of the hair and extracted very gently with precision instruments.

3

Graft Preparation

The extracted grafts are prepared for implantation by separating and preparing them according to the hair density required.

4

Follicle Implantation

Using Barley's microneedle implanter pen technology, the healthy grafts are precisely implanted in the recipient area.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about FUE hair transplant at Barley

💰 How much does FUE hair transplant cost?

FUE hair transplant costs at Barley typically range from $3,000 to $12,000 USD, depending on the number of grafts needed. The cost per graft is usually $3-$6. Average procedures cost $5,000-$8,000. We offer free consultations to provide personalized quotes.

🔬 What is FUE hair transplant?

FUE (Follicular Unit Extraction) is a hair transplant technique where individual hair follicles are extracted from the donor area (back and sides of the head) using a small motorized punch, then implanted in the balding areas. It leaves minimal scarring and has a faster recovery than traditional strip surgery.

⏱️ How long does FUE recovery take?

FUE recovery is relatively quick. Most patients return to work within 3-5 days. The donor area heals within 7-10 days. Transplanted hair sheds after 2-4 weeks, with new growth starting at 3-4 months. Full results are visible at 12-18 months post-procedure.

🔍 Does FUE leave scars?

FUE leaves minimal, virtually undetectable scars. The extraction sites are tiny (0.8-1.0mm) and heal as small dots that are barely visible, even with short hair. This is a major advantage over FUT (strip surgery), which leaves a linear scar.

🔢 How many grafts can FUE transplant in one session?

FUE can transplant 2,000-3,500 grafts in a single session, depending on the patient's donor supply and the area to be covered. The procedure typically takes 6-8 hours. For more extensive baldness, multiple sessions may be needed.

♾️ Are FUE results long-lasting?

Yes, FUE results are long-lasting. The transplanted hair follicles are taken from the DHT-resistant donor area at the back of the head. Once transplanted, these follicles will continue to grow naturally for life, providing lifelong coverage.
