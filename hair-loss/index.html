What You Need to Know About Hair Loss

Hair loss affects millions of people worldwide, causing distress and impacting self-confidence. While losing 50-100 hairs per day is normal as part of the natural hair growth cycle, excessive hair loss can signal underlying issues that require attention.

What Causes Hair Loss?

Understanding the root cause is essential for determining the most appropriate treatment approach

🧬

Genetic Factors

Androgenetic alopecia accounts for 95% of all hair loss cases, caused by genetic predisposition and DHT hormone.

  • Men: Receding hairline and crown thinning
  • Women: Overall thinning with preserved frontal hairline
  • Can begin as early as late teens or early 20s
⚖️

Hormonal Changes

Hormonal fluctuations can trigger temporary or permanent hair loss in both men and women.

  • Pregnancy and childbirth
  • Menopause
  • Thyroid disorders
  • Polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS)
💊

Medical Conditions

Certain medical conditions and treatments can cause hair loss as a side effect.

  • Alopecia areata (autoimmune)
  • Scalp infections
  • Cancer treatments
  • Blood pressure medications
🌿

Lifestyle Factors

Daily habits and environmental factors can contribute to hair thinning and loss.

  • Chronic stress and anxiety
  • Poor nutrition and crash diets
  • Excessive heat styling
  • Tight hairstyles (traction alopecia)

Types of Hair Loss

Different types of hair loss require different treatment approaches

Androgenetic Alopecia

Most prevalent form affecting both men and women. Progressive and follows predictable patterns classified by Hamilton-Norwood (men) and Ludwig (women) scales.

Telogen Effluvium

Temporary excessive shedding caused by stress, illness, or hormonal changes. Hair usually regrows within 6-9 months once the trigger is addressed.

Alopecia Areata

Autoimmune disorder causing patchy hair loss. Can progress to total scalp or body hair loss in severe cases.

Traction Alopecia

Caused by repeated pulling from tight hairstyles. Most common around hairline and temples. Can be long-lasting if not caught early.

Scarring Alopecia

Group of disorders that destroy hair follicles and replace them with scar tissue, resulting in permanent hair loss.

Other Types

Includes medication-induced hair loss, nutritional deficiencies, and hair loss due to medical treatments like chemotherapy.

Hair Loss FAQ

Common questions about hair loss causes, types, and treatments

What are the main causes of hair loss?

Hair loss can be caused by genetics (androgenetic alopecia), hormonal changes, medical conditions, medications, stress, nutritional deficiencies, and autoimmune disorders. Male and female pattern baldness are the most common types, affecting millions worldwide.

How do I know if my hair loss is long-lasting?

Genetic hair loss (androgenetic alopecia) is typically long-lasting and progressive. Hair loss from stress, illness, or medications may be temporary and reversible. A professional diagnosis at Barley can determine your hair loss type and recommend the most effective treatment approach.

When should I seek treatment for hair loss?

Early intervention is key. If you notice significant thinning, receding hairline, or bald patches, consult a specialist immediately. Barley's microneedle technology works effectively when there's still some donor hair available. Don't wait until hair loss is advanced - earlier treatment means better results.

Can hair transplant treat all types of hair loss?

Hair transplant is most effective for androgenetic alopecia (genetic hair loss) and traumatic hair loss. It's not suitable for active alopecia areata or scarring alopecia. Barley's surgeons will assess your condition during consultation to determine if you're a good candidate for transplantation.
