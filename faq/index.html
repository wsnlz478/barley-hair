Frequently Asked Questions

Hair transplantation is a surgical procedure that moves hair follicles from one part of the body (donor area) to a bald or thinning area (recipient area). The transplanted hair continues to grow naturally in its new location.

The duration varies depending on the number of grafts being transplanted. Typically, a hair transplant procedure takes between 4-8 hours. Larger cases may require a second session.

The procedure is performed under local anesthesia, so you should not feel pain during the surgery. Some mild discomfort or soreness may be experienced during the recovery period, which can be managed with medication.

FUT (Follicular Unit Transplantation) involves removing a strip of skin from the donor area, while FUE (Follicular Unit Extraction) extracts individual follicles. Barley uses advanced microneedle FUE for minimal scarring and natural results.

Microneedle hair transplant uses a specially designed 0.6-0.8mm implant pen to place follicles directly into the scalp. This technique creates smaller incisions, reduces recovery time, and provides more natural directional control compared to traditional methods.
