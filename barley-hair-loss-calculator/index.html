Understanding Hair Loss Stages

Male pattern baldness is commonly classified using the Norwood-Hamilton scale, which ranges from Stage 1 (no significant hair loss) to Stage 7 (advanced baldness). Understanding your current stage is the first step toward effective treatment.

At Barley, we offer solutions for all stages of hair loss, from early prevention to advanced restoration. Our experts will help you determine the most suitable treatment plan for your specific needs and goals.

Hair Loss Assessment FAQ

Understanding your hair loss stage and treatment options

How accurate is the hair loss calculator?

Our calculator provides a general assessment based on the Norwood-Hamilton scale for men and Ludwig scale for women. While it's a useful starting point, a professional consultation at Barley includes scalp microscopy, hair pull tests, and medical history review for a precise diagnosis and personalized treatment plan.

What are the different stages of hair loss?

Hair loss is typically classified into 7 stages (Norwood scale for men): Stage 1-2 (minimal recession), Stage 3-4 (noticeable thinning and recession), Stage 5-6 (significant balding), Stage 7 (extensive hair loss). Early stages respond well to medication, while advanced stages benefit most from Barley's microneedle transplant.

How many grafts will I need for my hair loss stage?

Graft requirements vary by stage: Stage 2-3 typically needs 500-1,500 grafts, Stage 4 requires 1,500-2,500 grafts, Stage 5-6 may need 2,500-4,000+ grafts. Barley's microneedle technology achieves 95%+ graft survival, meaning you get more growing hair per graft compared to traditional methods.

What's the next step after using the calculator?

Book a free consultation at Barley for professional assessment. Our surgeons will evaluate your donor area quality, hair loss progression, and treatment goals. You'll receive a personalized plan with exact graft count, pricing, and expected results. No obligation - just expert advice to help you make an informed decision.
